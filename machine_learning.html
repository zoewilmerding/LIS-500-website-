Project Statement

Our goal for the machine learning project was to understand first-hand how machine learning models are built, trained, and how design choices impact the accuracy of the systems...

After running into issues with the machine classifying the “neutral” emotion, we decided to incorporate clear, visible hand signals...

After receiving feedback on our model, we realized there were many issues with having Amanda take every photo...

We repeated the design cycle of collect, train, test, and adjust over and over until the machine was working just how we liked it...

When designing our model, we quickly found that a neutral expression was nearly impossible for the machine to accurately identify...

The process of creating the model made us extremely aware of the ethical decision-making behind AI development...

Project Scope

Classification: Determining Different Emotions
Range: 3 Emotions
Emotions Listed: Happy, Neutral, Sad
Image Samples: 200-300 images for each classified item

Reflections On Our Project & Unmasking AI by Joy Bolumawani

Our machine learning technology teaches the computer how to recognize three basic facial expressions...

We began by deciding which expressions to include. We chose happy, neutral, and sad...

One concept from the book that we discussed was the idea of “Coded Gaze”...

As Boulamwini discussed in her book, AI systems are never neutral, as every classification reflects a human decision...

As we continued to test our model, we saw that the system confidently misclassified subtle expression differences...

Building this small version of a machine learning tool prompted us to think critically about the ethical principles behind AI development...
